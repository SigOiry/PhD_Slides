Macrophyte Discrimination: Successes & Challenges

Key Success: Multispectral RS + ML effectively differentiate intertidal vegetation, even with similar pigments (e.g., seagrass vs. green macroalgae).

Core Mechanism: Relies on detecting subtle spectral variations (pigment proportions & concentrations).

DISCOV Algorithm – A Key Output:

  • Dynamic, adaptable, and open-source (GitHub).
  • Challenge: Initial underperformance with Rhodophyta due to limited training data.
  • Solution: Expanded training dataset significantly improved model accuracy & robustness.

Ongoing Challenge: Natural variability in pigment content (phenology, stress, intraspecies variation).

Future Direction: Continue expanding the training dataset (broader geographic/temporal range) to enhance algorithm generalizability.

Drone Technologies in Coastal Monitoring

Significance of UAVs (Drones):

  • Provide very high-resolution spatial data.
  • Offer flexible and on-demand deployment.
  • Crucial for capturing fine-scale habitat heterogeneity and rapid environmental changes.

Applications Demonstrated in Associated Research:

Aquaculture Monitoring: Estimating Kappaphycus alvarezii biomass & carrageenan (Nurdin et al., 2023).

Applications Demonstrated in Associated Research:

Oyster Farm Assessment: Mapping intertidal oyster farm structures, mesh bag sizes, & table heights (A. Román et al., 2023).

Drone and Satellite Interactions: A Powerful Synergy

Complementary Strengths:

  • UAVs: Unmatched spatial resolution, ground-truth validation, training data generation.
  • Satellites: Broad spatial coverage, consistent temporal monitoring for large-scale trends.

Example: The ICE CREAMS Model (Davies et al., 2024a,b):

  • Methodology: DISCOV (drone-derived) classifications used to train and validate ICE CREAMS (Sentinel-2 satellite) model.

  • Achievements:

    • Mapped intertidal seagrass across diverse European sites.
    • Revealed latitudinal gradients in seagrass phenology.
    • Tracked inter-annual trends in seagrass extent.

Key Benefit: This synergistic approach balances local accuracy with regional/global scalability, optimizing monitoring efforts.

Assessing Coastal Ecosystem Threats via RS

RS for Comprehensive Threat Monitoring: Addresses impacts of climate change (heatwaves), eutrophication, invasive species, and habitat fragmentation.

Key Tools & Techniques Used/Discussed:

  • Multispectral/Hyperspectral Imaging: Assessing vegetation health, species composition, stress indicators.
    • Chapter 4: Mapping invasive Gracilaria vermiculophylla.
    • Chapter 5: Quantifying heatwave impacts on seagrass using the Seagrass Heat Shock Index (SHSI).
  • LiDAR: Precise topographic data (elevation, slope) influencing species distribution.
  • Vegetation Indices (NDVI, SHSI): Early detection of ecosystem degradation.
  • Machine Learning: Advanced detection, classification, and predictive modeling.

Broader Application: RS data can be integrated into risk assessment frameworks (e.g., DAPSI(W)R(M)).

Outcome: Provides a holistic understanding of coastal ecosystem dynamics, crucial for informed management and conservation.

Future Perspectives: Seagrass Restoration

Critical Context: Widespread global decline of seagrass meadows due to anthropogenic pressures necessitates urgent and effective restoration efforts.

Restoration Challenges: Success is variable (e.g., Arcachon Bay - Laurent, 2024; initial survival, then significant mortality).

How Remote Sensing Can Revolutionize Restoration:

  • Site Selection: Mapping habitat suitability (water quality, depth, substrate) & assessing pressures to identify optimal locations.
  • Monitoring Success: Tracking changes in coverage, density, and health of restored meadows.
  • Adaptive Management: Providing data to refine techniques & improve long-term outcomes.

DISCOV Model’s Role in Seagrass Restoration:

  • Precise Classification: Aids in selecting sites with minimal competition.
  • Fine-Scale Monitoring: Detailed assessment of restored patches.

Upcoming Application: EU Project REBORN (Interreg Program):

DISCOV and ICE CREAMS will be applied to:

  • Track seagrass distribution across North-West Europe.
  • Identify at-risk areas and prioritize restoration interventions.
  • Support transboundary data sharing and collaborative management.

Overall Conclusion

  • This PhD advanced multi-scale RS techniques for characterizing intertidal vegetation.
  • Developed novel tools (DISCOV, SHSI) and provided key insights for:
    • Monitoring ecosystem health and biodiversity.
    • Understanding and quantifying threats (invasive species, heatwaves).
    • Guiding targeted conservation and restoration strategies.
  • The integration of drone and satellite data, powered by machine learning, offers robust solutions for managing complex and dynamic coastal ecosystems.
